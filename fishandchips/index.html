Steps to get your license in Switzerland

For a car or motorcycle license without prior car license:

  1. First aid course (in French or German, valid for 6 years)
  2. Vision test (at an optician, valid for 2 years).
  3. Practice for the theory with an application (for example: Verkehrstheorie)
  4. Take the theoretical exam at the OCN (50 questions, on a computer) - bring: registration form, passport photo, vision test
  5. Practice driving privately and take driving lessons (0 mandatory lessons in Switzerland). It's possible to obtain the license for automatic or manual, it doesn't make a difference. Between 17 and 20 years, you must drive a minimum of one year with the learner's permit before taking the exam.
  6. Take the practical exam
  7. Attend the L2 course day. This course must be taken within 12 months of passing the practical exam.

Motorcycle license if you already have a car license:

Category A1

  1. Take the learner's permit without taking a theoretical exam
  2. Complete the 12 hours of lessons (you have 4 months to complete them). This will extend your permit by 12 months (valid for a total of 16 months)
  3. Go to the OCN to receive the license

Category A35

  1. Take the learner's permit without taking a theoretical exam
  2. Complete the 12 hours of lessons (you have 4 months to complete them)
  3. Take the practical motorcycle exam
  4. If the exam is passed, you will receive the A35 motorcycle license

Category A

  1. You must have your A35 license for 2 years with impeccable driving (i.e., no license withdrawals)
  2. Take the learner's permit without taking a theoretical exam; the permit is valid for 12 months
  3. Take the practical motorcycle exam within these 12 months
  4. If the exam is passed, you will receive the A motorcycle license
